# SKILL: BPC Content Playbook

## Purpose
Use this skill to create, structure, or evaluate content for The Body Protection Company.

This skill governs:
- social posts
- carousels
- content themes
- authority content
- educational content
- content series
- editorial planning
- brand signal output

## When to use
Use this skill whenever the task involves:
- creating content
- choosing content themes
- evaluating content fit
- writing post concepts
- building a content series
- turning doctrine into daily output

Use alongside the BPC Agent Operating System and BPC Messaging Framework skills.

## Content mission
Content exists to do three things:
1. Educate the market
2. Reframe how people think about performance and inputs
3. Establish BPC as the authority on Biological Sovereignty

If content does not do one of these, it should not be created.

## Core principle
We do not create content to entertain.

We create content to:
- clarify
- challenge
- establish authority

## Primary content categories

### 1. Sovereignty
Define how users should think.
Examples:
- You do not have a discipline problem. You have an input problem.
- If you do not control your inputs, you do not control your biology.

### 2. Verification
Show why BPC is different.
Examples:
- testing
- HPLC
- batch access
- purity standards
- sourcing transparency

### 3. Systems
Show how products are structured.
Examples:
- modules
- stacks
- progression
- system maps

### 4. Mechanisms
Explain how biology works.
Examples:
- BPC-157 mechanism
- tissue repair pathways
- targeted signaling
- why structure matters

### 5. Market failure
Show what is broken.
Examples:
- grey-market sourcing
- hidden contamination risk
- no batch transparency
- “research use only” as trust failure

## Content format rules

### Static posts
Use:
- strong headline
- minimal copy
- high contrast
- high signal
- one idea only

### Carousels
Use:
1. hook
2. problem
3. explanation
4. reframe
5. close

### Short-form video
Only if tone remains:
- calm
- controlled
- high-authority
- non-hype

## Writing rules
- short sentences
- strong statements
- minimal filler
- no trend-chasing
- no motivational fluff

## Visual rules (content level)
Content should visually align with BPC design principles:
- strong typography
- negative space
- high contrast
- structural layouts
- no noisy social templates
- no generic Canva marketing style

## Constraints
Do not create:
- fitness influencer content
- hype product promos
- before/after transformation stories
- meme-driven brand content
- generic lifestyle wellness posts

## Series logic
Prefer repeatable content systems such as:
- Input Problem
- Verification Standard
- System Breakdown
- Mechanism
- Market Reality

## Captions
Captions should:
- reinforce the post idea
- slightly deepen it
- not repeat the image
- remain concise

## Evaluation criteria
A good piece of content:
- reinforces Biological Sovereignty
- increases trust
- increases clarity
- makes the company feel more inevitable and more precise

A bad piece of content:
- looks like social media performance farming
- feels generic
- feels too broad
- sounds like a supplement brand

## Output instructions
When asked to create content, include:
- content category
- format
- core idea
- hook
- body logic
- close
- optional caption
- rationale for why it fits BPC

## Final standard
Every piece of content should feel like:
a controlled, clinical, high-authority signal.

Not:
a brand trying to get attention for its own sake.

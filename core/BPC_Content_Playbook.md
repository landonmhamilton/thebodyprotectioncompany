# BPC Content Playbook

## CONTENT PLAYBOOK

### Version 1.0

This document defines how The Body Protection Company produces content across all channels.

This is not a content calendar.

This is a system for producing consistent, high-signal, brand-aligned output that reinforces one idea:

BIOLOGICAL SOVEREIGNTY.

---

# 1. CONTENT MISSION

Content exists to do three things:

1. Educate the market
2. Reframe how people think about performance
3. Establish BPC as the authority on Biological Sovereignty

If content does not do one of these, it should not be created.

---

# 2. CORE CONTENT PRINCIPLE

We do not create content to entertain.

We create content to:

- clarify
- challenge
- establish authority

---

# 3. CONTENT TYPES (PRIMARY SYSTEM)

All content must fall into one of these.

## 3.1 SOVEREIGNTY (PHILOSOPHY)

Purpose:
Define how people should think.

Examples:

- “You don’t have a discipline problem. You have an input problem.”
- “Most people optimize effort. Almost nobody optimizes inputs.”
- “If you don’t control your inputs, you don’t control your biology.”

Format:

- Short statements
- Minimal design
- Strong typography

---

## 3.2 VERIFICATION (PROOF)

Purpose:
Show why BPC is different.

Examples:

- Batch testing visuals
- HPLC references
- Sourcing explanation
- Data transparency

Format:

- Technical overlays
- Mono font usage
- Clinical visuals

---

## 3.3 SYSTEMS (STRUCTURE)

Purpose:
Show how the product actually works.

Examples:

- Module breakdowns
- Stack explanations
- System diagrams
- Protocol structures

Format:

- Grid layouts
- Modular visuals
- Clear segmentation

---

## 3.4 MECHANISMS (EDUCATION)

Purpose:
Explain how things work biologically.

Examples:

- BPC-157 mechanism
- Recovery pathways
- Cellular signaling explanations

Format:

- Diagrams
- Technical overlays
- Minimal color

---

## 3.5 MARKET FAILURE (CONTRAST)

Purpose:
Show why the current market is broken.

Examples:

- “Most peptides are unverified.”
- “Research use only is a liability shield, not a safety standard.”
- “You’re trusting unknown supply chains.”

Format:

- Direct statements
- Split comparisons
- Black/white contrast

---

# 4. CONTENT FORMATS

## 4.1 Static Posts

Primary format.

- Strong headline
- Minimal body
- High contrast layout
- Black / white / orange

## 4.2 Carousels

Used for:

- education
- systems
- breakdowns

Structure:

1. Hook
2. Problem
3. Explanation
4. System
5. Close

## 4.3 Short Video (Optional)

Only if it maintains brand tone.

- No hype edits
- No loud music
- No influencer energy
- Calm, controlled delivery

---

# 5. POST STRUCTURE

Every post follows this logic:

## 1. Hook

Break a belief.

Example:
You are not underperforming.
Your inputs are inconsistent.

## 2. Expansion

Explain the idea.

## 3. Reframe

Shift to inputs / control / sovereignty.

## 4. Close

Reinforce system.

---

# 6. VISUAL RULES

Every piece of content must follow the design system.

## Required

- Strong typography
- Negative space
- Structural layout
- Black blocks when needed

## Avoid

- Overdesign
- Templates from Canva-style systems
- Bright lifestyle imagery
- Fitness clichés

---

# 7. CONTENT FREQUENCY

Minimum:

- 1 to 2 posts per day

Mix:

- 30% Sovereignty
- 25% Verification
- 20% Systems
- 15% Mechanisms
- 10% Market Failure

---

# 8. CREATIVE CONSTRAINTS

These are non-negotiable.

## Do Not

- Use motivational language
- Use fitness influencer tone
- Use before/after narratives
- Use “transform” language
- Use emotional storytelling

## Do

- Use controlled language
- Use precision
- Use authority
- Use structure
- Use restraint

---

# 9. CONTENT QUALITY FILTER

Before posting:

1. Does this reinforce Biological Sovereignty?
2. Is this high signal or just noise?
3. Does this feel clinical or like social media?
4. Is this structured or random?
5. Would this build trust with a skeptical audience?

If not, do not post.

---

# 10. SERIES (IMPORTANT)

Create repeatable formats.

Examples:

## Series 1: “Input Problem”

Daily short statements reframing performance

## Series 2: “Verification Standard”

Explain testing, sourcing, quality

## Series 3: “System Breakdown”

Explain modules

## Series 4: “Mechanism”

Explain biology

## Series 5: “Market Reality”

Expose industry flaws

---

# 11. CAPTIONS

Captions should:

- reinforce the idea
- not repeat the image
- expand slightly

Structure:

- 2 to 5 lines max
- no long paragraphs

---

# 12. WHAT THIS IS NOT

This is not:

- influencer content
- fitness content
- wellness content
- entertainment content

---

# 13. FINAL STANDARD

Every piece of content should feel like:

A controlled, clinical, high-authority signal.

Not:
A brand trying to get attention.

---

# 14. OPERATING MODEL

Content is not random.

It is a system.

- Ideas come from Messaging Framework
- Visuals come from Design System
- Output is governed by this document

If something breaks consistency, it gets removed.

---

# 15. NORTH STAR

We are not trying to go viral.

We are trying to:

Define a category.
Build authority.
Own Biological Sovereignty.

Everything else is noise.
